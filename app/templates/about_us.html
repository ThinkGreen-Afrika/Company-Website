About Us

ThinkGreen Afrika is more than an outsourcing company; we are a tech-driven talent development and job placement company, fostering the growth of youth who are willing to change their jobs and develop entrepreneurial readiness. We train and outsource skilled talents and professionals, nurture them within our three-month accelerator programs, and connect them with top global organizations to fit into the workplace.

Our Vision

To become the global hub for entrepreneurship, fostering a culture of collaboration, mentorship, and lifelong learning.

Our Approach

We believe that sustainable development is an ethical responsibility and a strategic imperative for long-term sustainability by implementing human-centred design (HCD) in problem-solving and design-thinking, transitioning from traditional-based learning to a project-based model to meet industry and market needs.

Our Mission

To regenerate the next century of skilled professionals to support the African labour workforce.

Our Impact Themes

Climate Action

Climate change is a real and undeniable threat to our entire civilization.The effects are already visible and will be catastrophic unless we act now. Through education, innovation and adherence to our climate commitments.

Decent Work and Economic Growth

This SDG about "decent work and economic growth" and is one of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals which were established by the United Nations General Assembly in 2015

Partnerships for the Goals

Partnership for the goal aims strengthen the means of implementation and revitalize the global partnership for sustainable development. It connect people worldwide. In addition, global partnerships allow each to share information, technology, and resources.

Gender Equality

Is fifth of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals established by United Nations in 2015. The 17 SDGs recognize that action in one area will affect outcomes in others, and that development must balance sustainablities.

Quality Education

Empowering women and promoting gender equality is crucial to accelerating sustainable development. Ending all forms of discrimination against women and girls is not only a basic human right, but it also has a multiplier effect across all other development areas.
