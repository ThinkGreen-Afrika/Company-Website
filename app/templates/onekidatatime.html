1k@T

One Kid @ A Time Project

The One Kid At a Time's noble ambitions to become a digital literacy Hub of the East African region will become a reality, transforming itself from a cognitive and technical skills at a basic level to digital proficiency manpower with a call for producing excess capacity of digital literacy among youth 4-12th grade with market-oriented, and indemand skills.

Our Mission

To set a grassroots infrastructure for digital literacy from pre-school to high school.

Our Vision

Embraced digital literacy as a transformative, cutting-edge tool to support African education.

What Are We Solving?

Laying a grassroots, of cutting-edge digital literacy infrastructure for a sustainable education to support the Rwandan educational global economy.
While over the past decade, the education system has produced a commendable number of ICT professionals, there is a recurrent problem of a glaring mismatch between the supply and demand of ICT skills. This mismatch is attributable to a lack of strong linkages between, on one side, the outputs of HLI’s and other training institutions and, on the other side, the specific ICT skills needs of the ICT industry, which requires a more specialized, hands-on workforce. The mismatch between ICT skills and supply is even more evident at the postgraduate level (Master's and PhD), a segment that has the potential to supply experts who would help Rwanda transform itself from an importer to an exporter of ICTs. The number of postgraduate-level professionals remains very low.
